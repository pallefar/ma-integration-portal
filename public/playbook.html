HR M&A Guidebook

This guidebook provides a general overview of HR's role across the M&A process — outlining the phases, roles & responsibilities, and the resources available to HR. It offers high-level directional guidance for HR professionals with limited to moderate M&A experience.

No two transactions are the same, so expect variances in overall timeline, key tasks, data quality and team size. HR M&A partners closely with BU HR in an advisory capacity, guiding the team through each phase of the lifecycle.

Integrate with Integrity All M&A activity upholds TE's four core values — Integrity, Accountability, Teamwork and Innovation — turning them into step-by-step operational workflows.

1. Overview of M&A

Mergers & acquisitions are inorganic growth strategies that combine two companies to achieve synergy — where the whole is greater than the sum of its parts. As a large company, TE brings back-office advantages in purchasing, tax, distribution networks, markets, customers and product offerings.

The M&A Lifecycle

The lifecycle mirrors buying a house: Target Screening → Due Diligence → Integration Planning → Integration → Value Capture, with a non-binding offer (LOI) leading to Sign and Close. Prior to sign/close, HR spends the most time in Due Diligence — analyzing risk with imperfect information.

  • Deal type matters: in a stock deal the legal entity (with its assets, liabilities and employees) transfers; in an asset deal only specified assets/liabilities transfer.
  • Strategy drives HR: Decision #1 is whether the business is left standalone (no integration) or integrated — which shapes org design, retention and integration plans.

2. Investigate — HR Pre-Diligence

Before formal due diligence, HR M&A and BU HR conduct early analysis of the target's culture, values and leadership — using limited, often publicly available information. The goals are to assess TE organizational readiness, form a preliminary view of target leadership, and gain early insight into target culture.

Talent & Culture Executive Summary Prepare a high-level summary of organizational readiness, culture/talent fit and risk mitigants, and select an Integration Leader. State what success looks like for Talent and Culture 12 months post-integration.

3. Transaction — HR Due Diligence

If the deal proceeds, HR M&A kicks off formal due diligence with a small HR DD team that reviews the documents in the Virtual Data Room (VDR) to analyze risk and draw conclusions. There is much to do in a short period with imperfect information.

Goals of HR Due Diligence

  • Quantify the legacy and potential future costs TE could incur with the purchase.
  • Identify key employees and design retention approaches.
  • Operate with a OneHR mindset — HR M&A manages the process, SMEs review, and the team prepares the final HR DD report.

4. HR Integration Planning

The M&A thesis and BU strategy drive the integration approach needed to achieve synergy or deal-model return. The BU determines the level of integration: Standalone (preserve deal value) or Integrated (capture combined synergies).

"Big C" — Non-Negotiables Even standalone / "walled off" businesses must meet legally required compliance standards ("Big C") — the minimum level of integration for every acquisition. TE's Compensation Strategy (pay for performance, pay equity, pay transparency) is a TE Standard.
  • Establish the Integration Management Office (IMO) and identify functional integration leaders.
  • Align timing and deliverables to the HR Integration Framework — planning depth depends on whether the target is standalone/TSA (limited Day 1) or a carve-out (accelerated Day 1).

5. HR Integration Execution

Execution is the active phase where the earlier strategy and planning are implemented. The typical recommendation is to integrate as quickly as reasonable and generally align with existing TE policies, practices and benefits (where congruent with deal strategy), in accordance with local laws and works-council agreements. Some elements, such as payroll, may occur over a multi-year period.

  • Total Compensation: finalize the Global Job Framework (GJF) mapping and total compensation packages; confirm where acquired leaders sit in the TE structure.
  • Communications & Change Management: BU HR leads, partnering with Regional HR and COEs; run a stakeholder analysis and ensure HR messaging aligns with the broader BU plan.
  • Confirm timing to join TE's Performance Management schedule & system, and onboard acquired HR employees.

6. Post-Integration & Resources

After the myHR integration is complete, conduct a debrief to reflect on what went well and where to improve; where appropriate, run a pulse survey of acquired employees or managers. Some acquisitions have multi-year plans to fully align to TE standards.

Sample Resources

  • HR Integration Communications Plan (intro email, employee town hall, GJF letters, payroll).
  • GJF communication — the final mapping is delivered to each employee by their manager; some countries require a contract amendment or a job-confirmation letter.
  • Sample Close-to-Go-Live and Go-Live timelines (HR integrations generally go live Jan 1, Apr 1 or Jul 1).

7. Divestitures

At times TE determines a divestiture is the best strategic path. Each divestiture is unique; HR M&A aligns closely with Corporate M&A on timing, structure, confidentiality and other key elements, and leads BU HR through the process. A very limited number of people are brought "in the know," and confidentiality must be maintained throughout.

Divestiture Phases

  • Investigate & Data Room: confirm deal process/structure and build a robust data room of material documents buyers need to review.
  • Due Diligence: respond to selected buyers' questions and document requests.
  • Separation Planning & TSA: prepare to transfer employees and contracts at Close, and stand up a Transition Service Agreement (TSA) to minimize disruption (e.g. continued myHR access, payroll services).

5. Lessons Learned & Case Studies

Successful post-merger integrations are built on historical insights. Below are verified lessons learned and interactive mock case studies compiled from past TE Connectivity acquisitions and integrations.

Case Study A: Project Alpha (Industrial Sensor Acq.)

SUCCESSFUL SYNERGY

Background: TE acquired a niche manufacturer of smart pressure sensor capsules. The goal was to migrate their proprietary engineering designs into TE's global automotive sales catalog.

Critical Challenge: Acquired engineers feared losing creative freedom under a larger corporate reporting matrix, leading to initial resistance and delays in patent transfers.

💡 Key Lesson Learned: Do not impose product roadmaps unilaterally. The IMO established the "Co-Design Council", allowing acquired engineers to maintain design steering over custom sub-components while leveraging TE's global supply chain for scaling. Result: Attrition fell to zero, and catalog integration completed 12 days ahead of schedule!

Case Study B: Project Beta (European Systems Harmonization)

TALENT RETENTION

Background: Integration of a 400-employee manufacturing site across Germany and France, involving complete payroll migration and benefits restructuring.

Critical Challenge: Uncoordinated announcements about title re-banding caused severe anxiety, sparking a 12% drop in organizational sentiment scores during Month 1.

💡 Key Lesson Learned: Leverage localized HR leaders early. The IMO launched the "Buddy System" matching newly acquired site leads with experienced TE team members. The local site leaders were equipped with transparency toolkits regarding compensation structures. Result: Sentiment recovered from 2.6 to 4.5 by Day 90, and payroll was successfully unified.
