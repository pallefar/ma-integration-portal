HR M&A Guidebook

This playbook organizes TE's HR M&A guidance around four overarching integration themes — Strategy, Culture & Values, Talent & Systems and Value & Synergies — and connects each theme to the underlying HR M&A Guidebook. Use the survey metrics on the Dashboard to determine which themes need immediate focus.

No two transactions are the same, so expect variances in overall timeline, key tasks, data quality and team size. HR M&A partners closely with BU HR in an advisory capacity, guiding the team through each phase of the lifecycle.

Integrate with Integrity All M&A activity upholds TE's four core values — Integrity, Accountability, Teamwork and Innovation — turning them into step-by-step operational workflows.

1. Strategy

Every integration starts with the deal thesis. The M&A strategy — the type of acquisition and whether the business is left standalone or integrated — drives all HR org design, retention and integration planning. HR confirms the strategic rationale and expected value creation with BU leadership, then aligns the plan to achieve synergy or the deal-model return. Even standalone businesses must meet legally required compliance ("Big C").

2. Culture & Values

Aligned cultures foster innovation and teamwork; a gap in communication style or hierarchy is a primary cause of integration delay. HR assesses target culture early (often from limited information), prepares the Talent & Culture Executive Summary, upholds TE's values, and leads communications & change management through go-live and beyond. A post-integration debrief and pulse survey confirm what worked.

3. Talent & Systems

People are the core value of any acquisition. HR identifies and retains key employees during due diligence, confirms org design, maps the Global Job Framework (GJF) and total compensation, aligns performance management and benefits, and coordinates the systems transition (TEIS/ERP, myHR). The final GJF mapping is communicated to each employee by their manager.

4. Value & Synergies

Synergies are captured through disciplined execution and governance. The Integration Management Office (IMO) steers cross-functional workstreams against the milestone roadmap; HR integrates as quickly as reasonable — generally aligning to TE policies, practices and benefits where congruent with deal strategy — to realize back-office and go-to-market advantages. Some elements, such as payroll, may span multiple years.

5. Lessons Learned & Case Studies

Successful post-merger integrations are built on historical insights. Below are verified lessons learned and interactive mock case studies compiled from past TE Connectivity acquisitions and integrations.

Case Study A: Project Alpha (Industrial Sensor Acq.)

SUCCESSFUL SYNERGY

Background: TE acquired a niche manufacturer of smart pressure sensor capsules. The goal was to migrate their proprietary engineering designs into TE's global automotive sales catalog.

Critical Challenge: Acquired engineers feared losing creative freedom under a larger corporate reporting matrix, leading to initial resistance and delays in patent transfers.

💡 Key Lesson Learned: Do not impose product roadmaps unilaterally. The IMO established the "Co-Design Council", allowing acquired engineers to maintain design steering over custom sub-components while leveraging TE's global supply chain for scaling. Result: Attrition fell to zero, and catalog integration completed 12 days ahead of schedule!

Case Study B: Project Beta (European Systems Harmonization)

TALENT RETENTION

Background: Integration of a 400-employee manufacturing site across Germany and France, involving complete payroll migration and benefits restructuring.

Critical Challenge: Uncoordinated announcements about title re-banding caused severe anxiety, sparking a 12% drop in organizational sentiment scores during Month 1.

💡 Key Lesson Learned: Leverage localized HR leaders early. The IMO launched the "Buddy System" matching newly acquired site leads with experienced TE team members. The local site leaders were equipped with transparency toolkits regarding compensation structures. Result: Sentiment recovered from 2.6 to 4.5 by Day 90, and payroll was successfully unified.
