Post-Merger Integration Playbook

Welcome to the official **TE Connectivity Post-Merger Integration (PMI) Playbook**. This interactive reference is designed to help Integration Leaders and Integration Leads navigate the first 100 days of structural and cultural transitions.

Mergers and acquisitions succeed when organizational design, talent retention, and cultural values align seamlessly. Use the survey metrics on the Dashboard to determine which playbook tracks require immediate activation.

Integrate with Integrity At TE Connectivity, all M&A activities must uphold our four core values: **Integrity, Accountability, Teamwork, and Innovation**. This playbook transforms these values into step-by-step operational workflows.

1. Culture & Values Alignment

A critical gap in communication styles or organizational hierarchy is the primary cause of integration delays. Aligned cultures foster innovation and collective teamwork.

Key Integration Milestones:

Cultural Warning Flag If the survey Culture score falls below 3.0, schedule immediate, independent 1-on-1 feedback sessions with middle management. Frictions left unchecked create operational resistance.

Best Practice: Active Dialogue Sessions

Do not push TE Connectivity corporate procedures unilaterally. Instead, position the integration as a collaborative partnership. Frame decisions around joint technical capacity, emphasizing how TE's global supply chain acts as an accelerator for the acquired team.

2. Talent & Systems Onboarding

People are the core value of any acquisition. Retaining critical technical engineers, product developers, and client relationship leads must be prioritized immediately.

Talent Retention Lock-In Establish key talent lists within the first 10 days. Deliver retention bonus agreements, career pathway blueprints, and schedule individual 1-on-1s. Maintain a target of less than 5% critical talent attrition.

HR Systems Migration Checklist:

Handling Overlaps and Redundancies:

Where functional overlap occurs (e.g. Finance, Legal, HR teams), map team capabilities. Look to deploy overlapping high-performers into expanded global roles in TE. Focus on positive growth pathways.

3. Value & Synergies Capture

Every acquisition has a strategic business case. Synergies are captured by integrating technology, streamlining sales networks, and combining supply chains.

Synergy Core Steps:

1. **Strategic Synergy Calibration:** Ensure the acquired executive leadership team clearly understands and embraces the post-merger synergy growth goals. 2. **Technology Transfer:** Map product developments, patents databases, and share corporate R&D testing resources. 3. **Go-to-Market Alignment:** Harmonize pricing lists, merge CRM customer pipelines, and leverage TE's global shipping channels.

Growth Synergy Focus Synergies are not just about cost-cutting; focus heavily on cross-selling. Use TE's vast automotive and industrial sales pipelines to introduce the acquired products to new global markets.

4. IMO Operational Best Practices

The **TE Integration Management Office (IMO)** operates as the steering engine. To keep the project running seamlessly, the IMO utilizes:

  • **Weekly PMO Standups:** Align cross-functional tracks (HR, IT, Sales, Legal, Finance) against the milestone roadmap.
  • **Scorecard Audits:** Re-run survey diagnostics at Day 30 and Day 60 to verify that coaching plans are successfully mitigating risk.
  • **Confidential Compliance:** Ensure all transitional paperwork and payroll accounts are handled in accordance with strict TE regional guidelines.

5. Lessons Learned & Case Studies

Successful post-merger integrations are built on historical insights. Below are verified lessons learned and interactive mock case studies compiled from past TE Connectivity acquisitions and integrations.

Case Study A: Project Alpha (Industrial Sensor Acq.)

SUCCESSFUL SYNERGY

Background: TE acquired a niche manufacturer of smart pressure sensor capsules. The goal was to migrate their proprietary engineering designs into TE's global automotive sales catalog.

Critical Challenge: Acquired engineers feared losing creative freedom under a larger corporate reporting matrix, leading to initial resistance and delays in patent transfers.

💡 Key Lesson Learned: Do not impose product roadmaps unilaterally. The IMO established the "Co-Design Council", allowing acquired engineers to maintain design steering over custom sub-components while leveraging TE's global supply chain for scaling. Result: Attrition fell to zero, and catalog integration completed 12 days ahead of schedule!

Case Study B: Project Beta (European Systems Harmonization)

TALENT RETENTION

Background: Integration of a 400-employee manufacturing site across Germany and France, involving complete payroll migration and benefits restructuring.

Critical Challenge: Uncoordinated announcements about title re-banding caused severe anxiety, sparking a 12% drop in organizational sentiment scores during Month 1.

💡 Key Lesson Learned: Leverage localized HR leaders early. The IMO launched the "Buddy System" matching newly acquired site leads with experienced TE team members. The local site leaders were equipped with transparency toolkits regarding compensation structures. Result: Sentiment recovered from 2.6 to 4.5 by Day 90, and payroll was successfully unified.
